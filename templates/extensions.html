Why Browser Extensions Matter

Types of Privacy Extensions

  • Content Blockers

    Block ads, trackers, and malicious content. Examples: uBlock Origin, AdBlock Plus

  • Tracker Blockers

    Target tracking scripts specifically. Examples: Privacy Badger, Disconnect

  • Cookie Managers

    Control which cookies can be stored. Examples: Cookie AutoDelete, I don't care about cookies

  • Anti-Fingerprinting

    Prevent browser fingerprinting. Examples: Canvas Blocker, Privacy Possum

Impact on Performance

While privacy extensions are essential for protection, they can impact your browsing experience in different ways:

Memory Usage

Each extension consumes additional memory. Having too many extensions can slow down your browser, especially on devices with limited RAM.

Page Load Time

Content-blocking extensions analyze each page element before loading, which can increase the initial page load time.

Compatibility Issues

Some extensions may conflict with each other or break certain website features, requiring troubleshooting or adjustments.

Finding the Right Balance

Our compatibility checker helps you find the optimal combination of extensions that provides strong privacy protection without excessive performance impact or redundant functionality.
